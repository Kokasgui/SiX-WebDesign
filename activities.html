SiX: Activities Overview

The SiX Research Project is dedicated to enhancing communication and accessibility for individuals with Locked-in Syndrome (LIS). Through collaborative efforts, the project explores digital drawing as a communication tool, involving patients, caregivers, healthcare professionals, researchers, and students.

Exploratory phase

Led by Bruno Giesteira, this phase focuses on understanding the needs of individuals with LIS and their caregivers. Key activities include:

READ MORE

Observational Fieldwork

Researchers, under the guidance of therapist Filipe Gonçalves, will observe patients in therapeutic settings at APELA (Portuguese Association for Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis), focusing on cognitive, motor, and social interactions.

Stakeholder Workshops

These sessions will gather insights from caregivers and therapists involved in LIS care, promoting knowledge exchange.

User Interviews

Ethnographic interviews and shadow observations will explore the daily routines and challenges of LIS patients.

Preliminary Drawing Activities

Pilot drawing sessions will investigate how drawing can act as a communication medium in immersive digital settings, exploring themes like distant memory and spatial memory.
