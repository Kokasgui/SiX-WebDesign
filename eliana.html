Eliana Pereira dos Penedos Santiago

Eliana Santiago is an Assistant Professor of Design and Drawing at the Faculty of Fine Arts of the University of Porto (FBAUP). She completed her degree in Visual Communication Design in 1997 and began her career in web and interaction design at a pioneering e-commerce company. Her interest in Computational Media and Digital Grammars, Languages and Processes led her to a Master's degree in Multimedia Digital Arts in 2000. Santiago's teaching experience in higher education began in 2003 at Escola Superior de Artes e Design (ESAD). She later earned her PhD in Drawing from the Universidad Politécnica de Valencia, Facultad de Bellas Artes de San Carlos in 2012. Her dissertation, "El Arte del Azar: Incidencias del Acaso en el Arte Electrónico," explored the role of chance in electronic art.

Santiago is an integrated researcher at ID+, a research unit comprised of 12 working groups. Her work aligns with the Head group's focus on applied research in multidisciplinary contexts. She is also a collaborating researcher at i2ADS, working within the research program for Computation, Hybrid Practices and Culture

Santiago has extensive experience supervising postgraduate research, including one ongoing PhD thesis, 20 completed MA projects, and two ongoing projects. Many of these were developed in interdisciplinary settings with collaborations from researchers and experts from different fields of expertise, such as physicians, psychologists, and therapists. She has also coordinated and co-organised five seminars titled "MAIS DO QUE A SOMA" under the WT project (POCI-01-0145-FEDER-029038) and the Annual Design Research Forum since 2021 as part of the MA in Design for Health and Wellbeing (MDSBE). Santiago is regularly invited as a moderator and speaker at academic and artistic events on design education, creative coding, and computation in the arts and design.

Her research outputs in Design for Health have highlighted the impact of design research in health and laid the groundwork for integrating technology and computational design with health design research. These outputs include projects such as "Health & Design at Service of a Refugee Camp in Iraq," "Cohort Study Good Practices: Design Communication & Capacitation Processes," and "Designing for People with Dementia: A Portuguese Case Study."
