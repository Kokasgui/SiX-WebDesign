SiX is a research project that explores how digital drawing can help patients with Locked-in Syndrome (LIS) reconnect socially and express themselves. The project uses the human body as an interface, converting minimal physical movements into drawing commands. This approach recognizes that creative expression is a fundamental human need, particularly important for those with severe motor impairments.

Outputs

Events/Activities

SiX will host workshops and presentations for students, researchers, and healthcare professionals. These events will focus on:

Understanding LIS and its impact

Raising awareness of LIS and the challenges faced by patients and their families.

Digital drawing as a communication tool

Exploring the potential of digital drawing to facilitate communication and expression for LIS patients.

Designing accessible and inclusive technologies

Sharing insights into the design process and promoting the development of technologies that meet the needs of people with disabilities.

Research/Publications

The SiX project will produce a variety of publications, including:

Scientific papers

Published in peer-reviewed journals, disseminating the project's findings and contributing to the scientific understanding of LIS and assistive technologies.

Design guidelines and manuals

Providing practical guidance for designers and developers creating communication tools for LIS patients.

White papers and reports

Documenting the project's methodology, results, and recommendations.


These publications will bridge the gap between research and practice, encouraging the development of innovative solutions that improve the lives of LIS patients.
